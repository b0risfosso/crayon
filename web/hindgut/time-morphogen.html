Time as the Fourth Morphogen — Embryonic Temperature / Seed

Problem → Tissue mechanics may violate simple Arrhenius scaling; myosin kinetics vs cortical viscosity shift differently with T, derailing proportional germband extension • Objective → Preserve proportional morphogenesis across temperature • Link → Couple myosin turnover & tissue viscosity via Rho‑kinase feedback so activation energies match → thermo‑isochronous mechanical module tracking a global thermal clock

Core intent: If ROCK feedback couples myosin turnover to cortical viscosity so their effective activation energies match, a single dimensionless myo–viscous ratio stays temperature‑invariant, yielding proportional (thermo‑isochronous) germband extension across a global thermal clock.

Why This is a Box of Dirt

Reduces the idea to one invariant (slope matching → constant MVR) and one visual consequence (trace collapse on a thermal clock). No models, fits, or data ingestion—only mocked panels and prefilled traces—so it’s discardable if the core doesn’t resonate. Growth‑inviting: swap in real slopes, reporters, and kymographs; extend to other tissues/species or alternative feedback paths beyond ROCK.
