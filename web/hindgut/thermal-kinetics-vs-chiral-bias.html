Thermal Kinetics vs Chiral Bias — Hindgut Rotation / Seed

Problem → Observed Arrhenius slopes of ω(T) conflate activation energies from motors, turnover, and fluid drag • Objective → Deconvolve and assign activation energies that best explain ω(T) across temperatures • Link → ω(T) ∝ [kmyosin(T) · kturnover(T)] / η(T); fit across multi‑T datasets and cross‑validate with perturbations that reweight the contributions

Core intent: Test whether a composite Arrhenius decomposition — motor stepping × actin turnover divided by cytoplasmic viscosity — parsimoniously explains temperature dependence of rotation rate, separating thermal kinetics (rate magnitude) from chiral bias (rotation sign).

Why This is a Box of Dirt

Minimal: mocked datasets, fixed plots, and pre‑scripted parameter tweaks — no fitting engine, no data ingestion. Disposable: if separation fails or missing physics dominates, toss or refactor without sunk cost. Growth‑inviting: swap in in‑vivo η(T), add explicit chiral torque if sign varies with T, extend the composite model to adhesion/ECM friction — all while preserving identifiability logic.
