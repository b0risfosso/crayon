Polarity Fence — Hindgut / Seed

Problem → Basolateral determinants spill across the boundary during remodeling, eroding side‑specific identity • Objective → Side‑locked, non‑overlapping basolateral maps on each side of the Crumbs line • Link → Nanobody relocalization of Scrib/Lgl/Dlg to side‑restricted scaffolds + aPKC‑phosphomimic gates to enforce exclusion; monitor with shape metrics & aPKC biosensors

Core intent: If the Crumbs line acts as a functional polarity fence, then forcibly side‑locking basolateral determinants and gating aPKC exclusion should yield two non‑overlapping, independently stable basolateral identity maps that persist through hindgut remodeling.
