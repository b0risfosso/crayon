Chirality‑to‑Rotation Pipeline — Hindgut Mechanisms / Seed

Problem → Role of visceral muscle in converting epithelial chirality to organ twist is unresolved • Objective → Determine whether muscle transmits, amplifies, or damps chirality and quantify coupling • Link → Two‑layer torque‑balance with interfacial friction; layer‑specific perturbations and fits

Core intent: A minimal two‑layer torque‑balance with interfacial friction can explain how epithelial chirality yields whole‑organ rotation; the visceral muscle may transmit, amplify, or damp that chirality. We estimate the coupling’s sign and strength by comparing responses to muscle torque vs epithelial chirality flips.

Pre‑Scripted Scenes

1) Baseline Transmitter

  • Ce=+1.5, Tm=0, γ=medium, Se=Sm=medium, ends free
  • ωe≈+0.7, ωm≈+0.6, s small; Ω=+0.6; Jme>0; Role=Transmitter
  • Prediction: small +Tm ↑Ω linearly; −Tm ↓Ω

2) Low‑Friction Slippage

  • Ce as above; γ→low → ωe≫ωm; Ω≈+0.1
  • Jme≈0; Role=Decoupled (damped by slippage)
  • Prediction: muscle torque has little influence unless γ increases

3) Muscle as Damper

  • Ce=+1.5; Tm=−0.8; γ=medium → Ω≈+0.2
  • ωe reduced; ωm<0; Jme<0; Role=Damper
  • Prediction: |Tm| threshold reverses Ω; locate reversal to pin Jme
[Opposing arrows; partial cancellation]

4) Muscle as Amplifier

  • Ce=+1.5; Tm=+0.8; γ=medium → Ω≈+1.0
  • ωe, ωm both high; s small; Jme>0 strong; Role=Amplifier
  • Prediction: superlinear Ω vs Ce with +Tm
[Aligned long arrows]

5) Mosaic MyoID Flips

  • Clone fraction p=0.4 (right‑biased) → Ce,eff=+0.7; Tm=0; γ=medium; ends pinned
  • ωe heterogeneous; ωm low; position‑dependent slip; Ω small +
  • Prediction: if p>0.5 left‑biased, Ω flips sign unless +Tm offsets
[Shear patches; weak co‑rotation]

6) Wrap Compromised

  • Sm→0; γ→low; Ce=+1.5 → ωe>0, ωm≈0; Ω≈0
  • Jme≈0; Role=No transmission
  • Prediction: strong epithelial chirality with near‑zero Ω flags missing coupling

Why This is a Box of Dirt

Minimal: two rings + three knobs (Ce, Tm, γ) and Ω readout—no solver or data pipeline. Disposable: toss if the two‑layer framing or frictional coupling doesn’t land. Growth‑inviting: add anisotropy, spatial heterogeneity, end anchoring, viscoelasticity, or 3D geometry; replace scenes with fitted data; upgrade classifier to likelihood bands.
