Chiral Active Mechanics & Continuum Laws — Hindgut Twist / Seed

Problem → Discrete chiral rules (vertex‑level flows/tensions) and continuum torque laws are used in isolation → cross‑scale inference stalls • Objective → Build a multiscale dictionary linking vertex parameters to Cosserat couple stresses & odd elastic moduli • Link → Homogenize a chiral vertex lattice to extract effective spin inertia, couple‑stress, and odd elasticity; calibrate with ablation asymmetry & optogenetic flow reversals to infer coefficients that predict organ twist

Core intent: Show that a tiny, chiral vertex rulebook can be homogenized into a Cosserat/odd‑elastic continuum with a handful of coefficients, and that those coefficients—calibrated from two simple assays—predict the sign and scale of the hindgut’s stereotyped twist.

Why This is a Box of Dirt

Minimal: toy lattice, one‑page dictionary, and two prefilled assays—no FEM, sweeps, or data ingestion. Disposable: if sign & scale won’t cohere across assays, the dictionary is tossed without sunk cost. Growth‑inviting: deepen each box—derive coefficients for realistic cell shapes, add curvature‑couple terms for tubes, include heterogeneity & growth, or port to a full Cosserat‑rod simulation—once the cross‑scale link persuades first eyes.
