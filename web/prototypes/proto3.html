Women’s Health — Data, Privacy & Femtech / Seed

Problem → Broadband deserts & lack of private space • Objective → Confidential, reliable access points • Link → Solar kiosk + satellite + multilingual intake + navigator + offline‑first app

Core intent: offer a visibly private, trusted, low‑friction entry so women in bandwidth/space deserts actually complete a confidential visit and feel safe enough to return.

1) Discovery & Approach

Entrance cling “Private Women’s Health Visits Here” (no “telehealth”). Navigator greets softly and offers to help or wait outside.

Box‑of‑dirt prop
Foam‑core kiosk front with door, occupied light, and white‑noise speaker.

2) Entry & Consent

Language
Consent (8th‑grade)
  • OFF Save a copy to the cloud
  • OFF Share de‑identified data to improve services

What gets stored where: On this kiosk (temporary, encrypted) • On your phone (optional) • To clinician (only after you approve)

Quick Exit tile always visible → taps to weather screen and clears last screen.
[image placeholder — tablet UI with consent + Quick Exit]

3) Intake & Privacy Controls

  • Mock conversational intake: birth control refill, period concerns, pregnancy questions, STI test, pelvic pain, free text.
  • Headphones on hook; white‑noise on; sound meter card turns green when adequate.
  • Review Summary with per‑item toggles (Share/Don’t Share) + big Delete All button.
[image placeholder — intake cards & sound meter green]

4) Connection Simulation

Screen: “Securing a private line… Satellite link ~30–60s” + progress ring. If failure → info stays encrypted on kiosk until reconnect.
Door LED: Green = alone, Blue = navigator invited.
[video placeholder — prerecorded clinician greeting (“Hi, I’m Dr. Lee…”)]

5) Handoff & Follow‑up

[image placeholder — QR/NFC “Care Key” token]

Links visit to an offline‑first app. Example app state: “2 items waiting to sync when you’re on Wi‑Fi or at a kiosk.”

  • Printed card with local resources (disguised name for safety, e.g., “Community Wellness Hours”).
  • On‑screen scheduling: pickup at pharmacy counter; lab slip printed at desk (mocked).

6) Data Flow Overlay

Flow
  1. Intake → Local encryption (edge)
  2. Approve to Send
  3. Satellite backhaul
  4. Clinician portal
  5. Plan summary
  6. Optional sync to phone app
Failure & Safety
  • No satellite → store encrypted for 24h → return later or allow navigator to send → auto‑delete countdown shown.
  • Quick Exit • No call log on phone • Name masking • PIN/pattern to re‑enter session.
[image placeholder — flow arrows poster]

Measurement & Signals

Sliders (1–5)

  • Did you feel private?
  • Did you understand what was shared?
  • Would you recommend this?

Observer checklist

  • Door → Ready to Connect < 7 min (target)
  • Help requests; teach‑back comprehension
  • Quick‑exit used due to fear < 15%

Validation signals

  • Trust: ≥70% select 4–5 privacy
  • Control: ≥80% explain data flow, use ≥1 toggle
  • Completion: ≥60% reach “Ready” without staff inside
  • Willingness: ≥60% return/recommend; ≥50% take Care Key
