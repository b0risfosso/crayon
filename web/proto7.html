pglang — West Coast Systems Design / Seed

A (Problem): LA’s design, film, and fashion sectors operate in silos, limiting cross‑pollination and scale.
B (Objective): Deep collaboration across LA’s design, film, and fashion ecosystems.
Link: West Mesh Residency: seasonal cross‑discipline teams co‑develop a film / lookbook / installation capsule premiered jointly in Leimert Park and a downtown museum.

Core intent: If siloed crafts co‑create under one motif and deadline, can a single capsule feel unified—and locally rooted—enough for a dual premiere (Leimert Park + DTLA museum)?

Capsule v0 (described, not built)

  • Film — “Median Lines” (3:00): Six shots at an orange‑lit bus stop; dancer in tailored vest; fades follow gaffer’s dimmer curve; UI‑style overlays annotate street infrastructure.
  • Lookbook — “Hi‑Vis Romance” (12pp): 2×4 kerf translated to stitch patterns; spreads alternate street & studio; QR codes link to micro‑interactions.
  • Installation — “Sunset Grid”: Wood lattice w/ reflective piping; LEDs chase in S‑curve synced to film beats; viewer path taped like a crosswalk.

Why This is a Box of Dirt

  • Minimal: paper storyboard + printed console + curbside micro‑pop—no real residency, payroll, or venue contracts.
  • Disposable: artifacts can be recycled; if cohesion/demand is weak, sunset with minimal sunk cost.
  • Growth‑inviting: clear knobs (motif chips, budget tokens, timeline beats) scale to more disciplines, longer runs, or new neighborhoods; positive signals map to a pilot bill of materials.
