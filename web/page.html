Living Cities of Limestone — Coral Reefs / Seed

Problem → Restoration plans lack site‑specific climate baselines → resilience bets are guesswork • Objective → Read the city’s stone archives to guide what to plant, where, and how to build for longevity • Link → AI‑assisted sclerochronology on coral & coralline cores reconstructs centuries of local temperature, growth, and stress to tune broodstock and reef geometries

Core intent: If we can read centuries of local climate and stress from coral stone, we can make site‑specific restoration choices (what to plant, where, and how to build) that feel obviously right to practitioners and differ meaningfully across nearby sites.

Why This is a Box of Dirt

Minimal: mocked images, prewritten insights, scripted panels—no algorithms, no new data, no backend. Disposable: toss or rewrite after first‑eyes feedback with minimal sunk cost. Growth‑inviting: swap stock for real core scans; replace fake curves with lab‑derived series; plug in monitoring & cyclone logs; add genotype labels from eDNA; iterate geometry with a simple wave‑tank sim. The bet—stone archives yield site fingerprints that drive different, better choices—can be accepted or rejected from this tiny artifact alone.
